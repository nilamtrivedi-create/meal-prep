Day 1

THAI LETTUCE WRAPS

Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon neutral oil ~15mL
  • 2 cups Chili Crunch Chicken (page 7)
  • 3 tablespoons Serrano Garlic Sauce (page 9) ~45mL
  • Thinly sliced serrano peppers, for garnish
  • ¼ cup Peanut Sauce (page 6) ~59mL
  • 1 tablespoon warm water ~15mL
  • ¼ cup salted peanuts, chopped, divided ~40g
  • 12 to 14 butter or bib lettuce leaves
  • ½ cup julienned carrots
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced English cucumber
  • ¼ cup tightly packed cilantro
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced scallions
  • Optional: 1 cup Coconut Rice (page 8), warmed

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat neutral oil over high heat. Add the Chili Crunch Chicken , and cook, stirring occasionally, until hot and slightly caramelized around the edges, about 4 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, divide the Serrano Garlic Sauce between two ramekins and garnish with 2 to 3 slices of serrano peppers . In a small bowl, whisk the Peanut Sauce with water until smooth, then divide between two ramekins and garnish with ½ of the chopped peanuts .
  3. Arrange the butter lettuce , carrots , sliced cucumbers , cilantro , and scallions on two plates. Arrange the chicken alongside the lettuce and garnish with the remaining ½ of the chopped peanuts . Serve each plate with the prepared Peanut Sauce and Serrano Garlic Sauce. Notes: ● To eat , spoon the chicken into the lettuce and top with vegetables, peanuts, and sauces. ● For a more filling dinner, serve with Coconut Rice on the side.

PEANUT CRUNCH NOODLES

Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil, divided ~30mL
  • 1 shallot, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced ~12g
  • 1 cup julienned carrots
  • Kosher salt
  • 2 (2.5-ounce) ramen noodle cakes ~142g
  • ½ cup Peanut Sauce (page 6) ~118mL
  • ⅓ cup reserved noodle water ~79mL
  • 1 cup Chili Crunch Chicken (page 7)
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced serrano peppers
  • 2 tablespoons thinly sliced scallions
  • ¼ cup tightly packed fresh cilantro
  • ¼ cup peanuts, roughly chopped ~40g
  • Optional: 2 Spicy Soy Marinated Eggs (page
  • 10), sliced in half

Instructions

  1. Bring a large saucepan of water to a boil. Meanwhile, in a large sauté pan or seasoned wok, heat 1 tablespoon of neutral oil over medium-high heat. Add the shallot and cook, stirring frequently, until soft and translucent, 3 to 4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook, stirring until fragrant, 15 to 20 seconds.
  2. Reduce the heat to medium and add the carrots , stirring to combine. Cook until tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. Season the boiling water generously with salt , then add the ramen noodles and cook according to package instructions. Reserve ⅓ cup of water before draining.
  4. Add the noodles to the pan with the shallots and carrots, along with the Peanut Sauce and reserved noodle water . Toss vigorously until glossy and emulsified.
  5. Meanwhile, wipe out the saucepan and return it to high heat. Add the remaining tablespoon of neutral oil , then add the Chili Crunch Chicken . Cook, stirring occasionally, until hot and slightly caramelized around the edges, about 4 minutes.
  6. Divide the peanut noodles between two bowls and top with the Chili Crunch Chicken. Garnish with serranos , scallions , cilantro , chopped peanuts , and if using, Spicy Soy Marinated Eggs .
Day 3

CRISPY SESAME CHICKEN BOWLS

WITH COCONUT RICE
Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 (9-ounce) boneless skinless chicken breast, sliced in half lengthwise ~255g
  • ¼ cup Spicy Soy Marinade (page 10) ~59mL
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour ~43g
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 egg ~57g
  • ⅔ cup panko breadcrumbs ~40g
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • Neutral oil
  • 1 tablespoon full-fat mayonnaise
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • ⅛ teaspoon brown sugar
  • ⅛ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 cup shredded purple cabbage
  • 2 tablespoons thinly sliced scallions
  • 2 tablespoons thinly sliced serrano peppers
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced English cucumber
  • 2 cups Coconut Rice (page 8), warmed
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Optional: 2 Spicy Soy Marinated Eggs (page 10), halved

Instructions

  1. Lay the chicken breast on a cutting board and cover with a large piece of parchment paper. Using a meat mallet or a heavy-bottomed skillet, pound the chicken until ½-inch thick. Transfer it to a bowl and cover with the Spicy Soy Marinade . Cover and let sit for 20 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, arrange three shallow bowls. In the first bowl, combine the flour and a pinch of salt . In the second, whisk the egg until smooth. In the third, mix the panko , sesame seeds , and a pinch of salt.
  3. Remove the chicken from the marinade and lightly pat dry before dredging. Dredge the chicken in the flour, shaking off any excess. Dip into the egg mixture, turning to coat both sides, then transfer to the breadcrumb mixture and press firmly to adhere the crumbs and prevent bald spots.
  4. Heat a ¼-inch layer of neutral oil in a large cast-iron skillet over medium heat until shimmering. Add the chicken and cook until deeply golden and crisp, 3 to 4 minutes per side. When the internal temperature reaches 165ºF, transfer to a paper towel–lined plate and season lightly with salt. Rest for 3 to 4 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, in a mixing bowl, whisk together mayonnaise , lime juice , brown sugar , and salt until smooth. Add the cabbage , scallions , serrano peppers , and cucumber and toss until fully coated.
  6. Divide the Coconut Rice between two bowls and top each with the crispy sesame chicken and the cabbage slaw. Garnish with fresh cilantro and Spicy Soy Marinated Eggs , if using.

THAI CHICKEN PIZZA

Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • ½ pound store-bought pizza dough
  • All-purpose flour, for dusting
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil ~15mL
  • ¼ cup Peanut Sauce (page 6) ~59mL
  • 1 cup Chili Crunch Chicken (page 7)
  • 2 ounces mozzarella or Oaxaca cheese, shredded (about ⅓ cup)
  • ½ cup julienned carrots
  • ¼ cup tightly packed fresh cilantro
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced scallions
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced serrano peppers
  • ¼ cup peanuts, roughly chopped ~40g

Instructions

  1. Let the pizza dough rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes and up to 1 hour.
  2. Preheat the oven to 500ºF. Arrange a rack in the top position. Place a large cast-iron skillet in the oven to preheat.
  3. Gently stretch the pizza dough on a lightly floured surface into a circle, about the same size as the bottom of the skillet.
  4. Carefully remove the cast-iron skillet from the oven. Drizzle olive oil into the skillet, then transfer the dough, arranging it so it slightly rises on the sides. Spread the Peanut Sauce over the top of the pizza dough, leaving a ½-inch border. Top with Chili Crunch Chicken , mozzarella , and carrots . Bake for 10 to 12 minutes, until golden brown.
  5. When the dough is finished, remove it from the oven and transfer it to a cutting board. Top with the cilantro , scallions , serranos , and chopped peanuts . Cut into 4 slices and serve.
Day 5

CHICKEN SATAY SALAD

WITH CRISPY COCONUT RICE
Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ tablespoons neutral oil, divided ~45mL
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder ~5mL
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon granulated garlic ~1mL
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more as needed ~5mL
  • 1 (9-ounce) boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces ~255g
  • 4 (8-inch) wooden or metal skewers
  • 1 cup Coconut Rice (page 8)
  • ¼ cup Serrano Garlic Sauce (page 9) ~59mL
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil ~45mL
  • 12 to 15 butter lettuce leaves
  • 1 cup shredded purple cabbage
  • ½ cup diced English cucumbers
  • ½ cup julienned carrots
  • ¼ cup peanuts, roughly chopped, plus more for garnish ~40g
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced scallions
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced serrano peppers
  • ¼ cup tightly packed cilantro, roughly chopped, plus more for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a small mixing bowl, combine 1 tablespoon of neutral oil , lime juice , curry powder , brown sugar , granulated garlic , and salt . Whisk until smooth. Add the chicken, tossing to coat, then cover and marinate at room temperature for at least 20 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 400ºF.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the Coconut Rice and 1 ½ tablespoons of neutral oil . Toss to combine, then spread onto the unlined baking sheet. Bake for 15 to 18 minutes, until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Meanwhile, thread the chicken onto the skewers. Heat the remaining tablespoon of neutral oil in a large oven-proof skillet over high heat. Add the skewers and cook, undisturbed, until lightly charred. Flip and continue to cook for 3 to 4 minutes. Transfer the skewers to the oven to finish cooking, about 5 minutes.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, whisk the Serrano Garlic Sauce and olive oil until smooth. Add the lettuce , cabbage , cucumbers , carrots , peanuts , scallions , serranos , cilantro , and crispy rice. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  6. Divide between two bowls and top each with 2 chicken skewers. Garnish with more cilantro and chopped peanuts .

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water ~237mL

  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt ~5mL

Instructions

SPICY SOY MARINADE

¾ cup of marinade

Ingredients

  • ½ cup (125g) soy sauce
  • ¼ cup (60g) warm water
  • 2 tablespoons (35g) honey
  • 1 tablespoon (10g) sriracha
  • For Spicy Soy Marinated Eggs
  • 4 large eggs ~228g
  • ⅓ cup Spicy Soy Marinade (see above) ~79mL

Instructions

  1. In a pint deli container, combine soy sauce , warm water , honey , and sriracha . Mix until the honey is fully dissolved. To Prepare the Spicy Soy Marinated Eggs
  2. Bring a small pot of water to a rolling boil on medium-high heat. Using a spoon, carefully drop in the eggs and set a timer for 8 minutes.
  3. In the meantime, fill a bowl with a 1:1 ratio of water and ice.
  4. Once the eggs are done boiling, transfer them to the ice bath to cool. Peel them in the water to ensure a smooth exterior.
  5. Pour ¼ cup of the Spicy Soy Marinade (roughly half the batch) over the peeled eggs, then cover. Refrigerate for up to 24 hours. To prevent a rubbery texture, remove the eggs from the marinade after 24 hours, and keep refrigerated.
