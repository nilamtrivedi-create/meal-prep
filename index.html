GRILLED CHICKEN CAESAR SALAD

WITH BOUILLON CROUTONS
Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 (9-ounce) boneless skinless chicken breast, sliced in half lengthwise ~255g
  • Kosher salt
  • ½ batch Lemon Oregano Marinade (page 6) ~55g
  • Neutral, high-smoke point oil, for cooking
  • 6 to 7 cups chopped romaine (from 2 hearts) ~492g
  • ⅓ cup Casey’s Caesar Dressing (page 9), plus more to taste
  • 4 ounces parmesan cheese, shaved & grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ lemon, cut into wedges, for serving ~55g
  • 3¾ cup Bouillon Croutons (page 10), for serving (see Note)

Instructions

  1. Place the chicken breast in a small bowl and add a generous pinch of salt. Mix well. Stir in the ½ batch of Lemon Oregano Marinade, fully coating the chicken, and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes (see Notes).
  2. Preheat the grill to 400ºF.
  3. Lightly coat the grill grates with a neutral, high-smoke point oil, and place the marinated chicken directly onto the grates. Cook for 11 to 12 minutes, flipping halfway.
  4. Meanwhile, in a large bowl, toss the romaine and Caesar Dressing until coated. Take a taste and decide if you need another tablespoon of dressing.
  5. Use a vegetable peeler to shave about 2 ounces of parmesan in long strips directly into the bowl, then switch to a rasp (see Notes) grater to grate the remaining 2 ounces of parmesan. Toss once or twice to make sure the cheese gets evenly distributed, then add a ton of good cracks of black pepper.
  6. Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for 4 to 5 minutes, before slicing.
  7. Plate the salad and place the sliced chicken directly on top. Serve immediately with lemon wedges for squeezing, and topped with Bouillon Croutons. Notes: ● For a more concentrated flavor, cover the marinated chicken with plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 4 hours (the longer, the better). ● A “rasp” grater is a generic term for a microplane. Since we prepped the cheese beforehand, use a generous handful of grated parmesan for this step.
Note● For a more concentrated flavor, cover the marinated chicken with plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 4 hours (the longer, the better). ● A “rasp” grater is a generic term for a microplane. Since we prepped the cheese beforehand, use a generous handful of grated parmesan for this step.
Day 2

ARAYES (MEAT-STUFFED PITA POCKETS)

WITH TOMATO CUCUMBER SALAD
Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • ½ pound ground chicken, beef, or lamb
  • 1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon chopped dill, divided
  • 1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon chopped parsley, divided
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, plus more for cooking ~15mL
  • Juice of ¾ lemon, divided
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin ~5mL
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste ~5mL
  • ½ teaspoon granulated garlic ~2mL
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder ~2mL
  • ¼ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes ~1mL
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more to taste ~1mL
  • A pinch of cinnamon
  • 2 Fluffy Pitas (page 11) (see Note)
  • 8 grape tomatoes, sliced in half
  • 2 cups diced Persian cucumbers
  • Tzatziki (page 12), for dipping
  • Silky Hummus (page 8), for dipping

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400ºF.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine ground meat, 1 tablespoon dill, 1 tablespoon parsley, 1 tablespoon olive oil, juice of ½ lemon, cumin, 1 teaspoon salt, granulated garlic, onion powder, chili flakes, ¼ teaspoon pepper and cinnamon. Stir until well combined.
  3. Wrap fluffy pitas in a slightly damp paper towel and microwave for 15 seconds–this will help ease the process of creating a pocket (see Note). Cut each fluffy pita in half and spread the meat filling evenly in the pita pockets. Brush the outside of each stuffed pita with olive oil and set aside.
  4. Heat a small glug of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Place the stuffed pita in the preheated skillet and cook on both sides for 2 to 3 minutes, or until toasted and golden brown. Finish searing with the meat side facing down for 1 minute.
  5. Transfer the arayes to a wire rack-lined sheet tray and bake for 4 to 5 minutes, or until the meat filling reaches an internal temperature of 165ºF.
  6. In a small mixing bowl, combine tomatoes, cucumbers, 1 teaspoon parsley, 1 teaspoon dill, salt, black pepper, ¼ lemon juice, and enough olive oil to lightly coat everything. Stir until well combined and set aside.
  7. Plate the cooked arayes with the tomato and cucumber salad on the side and serve with a dollop of tzatziki and hummus for dipping.
Day 3

LEMON OREGANO CHICKEN SKEWERS

WITH SUN-DRIED TOMATO GREENS
Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 (9-ounces) boneless skinless chicken breast, diced into 1½- to 2-inch cubes ~255g
  • Kosher salt
  • ½ batch (or remaining) Lemon Oregano Marinade (page 6)
  • 2 Fluffy Pitas (page 11)
  • 2- by 12-inch wooden or metal skewers
  • 1½ cups thinly sliced lacinato kale
  • 1½ cups chopped romaine lettuce
  • 8 grape tomatoes, sliced in half
  • 2 cups diced Persian cucumbers
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Sun-Dried Tomato Vinaigrette (page 7), to taste
  • Silky Hummus (page 8), for serving
  • Optional: Tzatziki (page 12), for serving

Instructions

  1. Place the diced chicken breast in a small bowl and add a generous pinch of salt. Mix well. Stir in the Lemon Oregano Marinade, fully coating the chicken, and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes (see Note).
  2. Preheat the oven to 450ºF.
  3. Wrap the Fluffy Pitas in aluminum foil and place directly in the oven to reheat–set a timer for 15 minutes.
  4. Thread the marinated chicken onto both skewers, about 8 to 9 pieces per skewer. Place the chicken skewers on a baking sheet topped with a wire rack and bake for 13 to 14 minutes, flipping halfway.
  5. Meanwhile, in a large mixing bowl, combine kale, lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, a pinch of salt, and black pepper. Toss until well combined, then add enough Sun-Dried Tomato Vinaigrette to coat everything. Toss well and set aside.
  6. Remove the fluffy pitas from the oven and set aside (keep them wrapped in foil to stay warm).
  7. Switch the oven to broil and finish cooking the chicken skewers for about 2 minutes, or until slightly charred and the internal temperature reaches 165ºF.
  8. Transfer the chicken skewers to a plate and allow them to rest for 5 minutes before serving.
  9. To serve, spoon a generous spoonful of Hummus onto each plate. Plate the sun-dried tomato greens to the side of the hummus and top each plate with a chicken skewer. Cut the warm pita into quarters and serve on the side with (optional) Tzatziki.
Day 4

PARMESAN CHICKEN CUTLETS

WITH SUN-DRIED TOMATO CAESAR SLAW
Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 (9-ounces) boneless skinless chicken breast, sliced in half lengthwise ~255g
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour ~32g
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 egg ~57g
  • ⅓ cup panko breadcrumbs ~20g
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Neutral, high-smoke point oil, for cooking
  • 1½ cups (or remaining) thinly sliced lacinato kale
  • 1½ cups (or remaining) chopped romaine lettuce
  • 8 grape tomatoes, sliced in half
  • 2 tablespoons Sun-Dried Tomato Vinaigrette (page 7)
  • ¼ to ⅓ cup Casey’s Caesar Dressing (page 9), plus more to taste
  • Shaved Parmesan, for garnish
  • 1¼ cup (or remaining) Bouillon Croutons (page 10)
  • ½ of a lemon, cut into wedges ~55g

Instructions

  1. Pat the chicken breast dry with a paper towel before placing it in a shallow bowl. In the same shallow bowl, coat the chicken with flour and a pinch of salt.
  2. In a separate shallow bowl, whisk the egg; in a final shallow bowl, combine panko, parmesan, a generous pinch of salt, and pepper.
  3. Coat the chicken breasts in flour, then place in the egg mixture, fully coating both sides. Transfer the egg-coated chicken breast into the breadcrumb-parmesan mixture, pressing the breadcrumbs onto the surface of the chicken.
  4. Heat enough neutral oil to cover the bottom of a large cast-iron skillet over medium heat (oil filling ¼ inch up the side of the pan preferred).
  5. Once the oil reaches 375ºF-400ºF, add chicken cutlets. Cook cutlets on both sides until golden brown and crispy, around 3 to 4 minutes per side. Once the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165ºF, transfer each cutlet to a paper towel-lined plate and season with a pinch of salt. Allow the chicken to rest for 3 to 4 minutes before serving.
  6. Meanwhile, in a medium mixing bowl, combine kale, romaine, tomatoes, salt, pepper, Sun-Dried Tomato Vinaigrette, and Caesar Dressing (add enough to fully coat the greens). Toss until well combined.
  7. Plate the chicken cutlet and top with the sun-dried tomato caesar slaw. Garnish with shaved parmesan, freshly ground black pepper, Bouillon Croutons, and lemon wedges for squeezing.

SUN-DRIED TOMATO GNOCCHI

WITH PARMESAN MEATBALLS
Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Sun-Dried Tomato Velouté (page 13)
  • 10 to 16 Parmesan Meatballs (page 14)
  • Optional: 1 to 2 tablespoon chicken or vegetable stock (see Note)
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 pound of potato gnocchi
  • 8 grape tomatoes, sliced in half
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan
  • Shaved Parmesan, for garnish
  • Chopped parsley, for garnish
  • Freshly ground black pepper, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of water up to a boil.
  2. Meanwhile, place Sun-Dried Tomato Velouté and Parmesan Meatballs in a saucepan over medium-high heat and bring the sauce to a light boil. Once the sauce is lightly bubbling on the sides of the pan, reduce the heat to low and stir. Cook just until the meatballs are fully reheated (see Note).
  3. Season the boiling water with a generous pinch of salt, then add the gnocchi. Cook according to package instructions, likely around 2 minutes.
  4. Stir the halved tomatoes, grated Parmesan, and cooked gnocchi into the sun-dried tomato velouté and add a small pinch of salt.
  5. Plate the gnocchi and garnish with shaved parmesan, chopped parsley, and a few cracks of freshly ground black pepper. Note: ● If the sauce appears too thick, stir in 1 to 2 tablespoons of stock–the sauce should be the same thickness as gravy or a quality queso.

SILKY HUMMUS

1 heaping cup

Ingredients

  • Juice of 1 lemon (1½ tablespoons)
  • 1 garlic clove ~4g
  • 1 (15.5-ounce) can chickpeas, drained & rinsed ~439g
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt, divided ~5mL
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda ~1mL
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cumin ~1mL
  • 2 cups water ~474mL
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 1 ice cube (the secret to extra-fluffy hummus)

Instructions

  1. Pour the lemon juice into a small bowl. Grate the garlic clove directly in the bowl and set aside.
  2. Pour the chickpeas into a colander. Rinse the chickpeas with cold water while shaking the colander until the cloudy foamy water washes off and the water is totally clear. (If your sink has a hose, now is the perfect time to use it.) Shake the colander again to drain, then pour the chickpeas into a large saucepan. Add ½ tsp of the salt, the baking soda, cumin, and water.
  3. Set over high heat until boiling, then reduce to medium-low and set a timer for 20 minutes. No need to stir or cover, just let them simmer away. When the timer goes off, carefully scoop out 1 tablespoon of the chickpea water and set that aside. Pour the chickpeas back into the colander to drain and let them sit for about 10 minutes until they’re cool enough to handle (see Note).
  4. Scoop up a handful of chickpeas. Pinch off the skin of each one. Skins go in the trash; naked chickpea in a food processor. (If some skins end up in the processor, totally fine. Don’t sacrifice a finger for the cause.) Hold a small mesh strainer over the food processor and pour the lemon juice through, discarding the garlic. Add that reserved chickpea water along with the tahini, ice cubes, and remaining ½ tsp of salt.
  5. Puree the mixture for 2 minutes until it’s broken down into a gorgeously thick mixture. Use a rubber spatula to scrape down the sides and taste for seasoning. Then process for 2 more minutes, until it’s unbelievably silky smooth. Note: ● This is where it gets a smidge tedious, but just for a moment! It’s worth it if you want to put in the effort, but the world will not stop turning if you skip it. ● To Store, transfer to an airtight container and store in the refrigerator for later use.
Note● This is where it gets a smidge tedious, but just for a moment! It’s worth it if you want to put in the effort, but the world will not stop turning if you skip it. ● To Store, transfer to an airtight container and store in the refrigerator for later use.

BOUILLON CROUTONS

5 cups

Ingredients

  • 1 (16-ounce) loaf soft Italian or French bread ~454g
  • ¼ cup olive oil ~59mL
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable or chicken bouillon paste (like Better Than Bouillon)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F. Line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Tear the bread into roughly 1-inch pieces with irregular edges. Arrange the croutons on the prepared baking sheet. In a small bowl, whisk the olive oil and bouillon together, then drizzle it over the croutons and toss to coat.
  3. Slide the baking sheet into the oven and set a timer for 4 minutes. Flip the croutons over, then set a timer for 4 more minutes. At this point they should be a little crunchy on the surface but soft inside. Give them 2 more minutes if you think they need it, then set aside to cool on the baking sheet. Note: ● To Store, place cooled-down croutons in an airtight container and store in a cool dark place for later use.

FLUFFY PITA

8 pitas

Ingredients

  • 2¾ cups (385g) unbleached bread flour, divided
  • 1 (0.25-ounce) packet instant yeast ~7g
  • 1 tablespoon honey or granulated sugar
  • 1 cup warm water ~237mL
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, plus more for the bowl ~15mL
  • 2 teaspoon kosher salt ~10mL

Instructions

  1. In the bowl of a stand mixer, whisk together 3⁄4 cup (105 grams) flour, the yeast, honey, and 1 cup (230 grams) warm tap water. Fit the dough hook to the mixer and add the olive oil and another 1 cup (140 grams) flour to the mixer bowl. Mix on low speed until a sticky dough forms, about 2 minutes. Scrape down the sides, then add the salt and the remaining 1 cup (140 grams) flour. Start mixing on low to get the flour incorporated, then increase the speed to high and set a timer for 5 minutes. The dough should be smooth, a little sticky, and the sides of the bowl should be clean.
  2. Lift the dough up, drizzle a little olive oil in the bowl, and plop the dough back in. Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap and put it in a warm place to rise. Set a timer for 1 hour and take a break. By the time you come back the dough should be beautifully puffy and doubled in size. If not, set a timer for 30 minutes and check again.
  3. Lightly dust half the counter with flour. Punch the dough down and scrape it out onto the floured side of the counter. Pat it into a rough rectangle and use a knife or a bench scraper to cut the dough into 8 equal pieces. With each piece, create a smooth ball by tucking the edges under. Set the ball on the unfloured side of the counter, seam side down, and lightly cup your hand over it with your thumb and pinkie firmly on the counter. Roll, using the heel of your hand, thumb, and pinkie as barriers against the ball, then place the ball back on the floured side. Keep rolling and space the balls about 2 inches apart on the counter.
  4. Sprinkle a little more flour on top of the balls and cover loosely with plastic wrap. Set a timer for 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat the oven to 500°F and set a rack in the lowest position. Set an upside-down baking sheet or a baking stone on the rack to preheat.
  6. Lightly dust the other side of the counter with more flour. Working one at a time, remove a ball and use you palm to press it flat into a small circle. (Keep the other balls covered until it’s their turn.) Grab a rolling pin or wine bottle and roll into a rough and imperfect 5-inch circle, dusting with more flour to prevent any sticking. Roll out 2 of the balls, then set a timer for 10 minutes to let them air dry. Roll out 2 more balls while those are resting and let them start drying.
  7. When the timer goes off, roll out the last 4 balls and let them dry out. Slide the low rack out and lift the first two circles on the preheated sheet. Immediately shut the oven and set a timer for 2 minutes. Use a wide spatula to flip the pitas and bake for 2 more minutes just so the bottoms can brown. Immediately remove and wrap in a thick kitchen towel to keep warm. Continue baking the rest of the pitas in the order that you rolled them.

SUN-DRIED TOMATO VELOUTÉ

2 cups

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons (42g) salted butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil ~15mL
  • 1 shallot, finely diced or thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, grated ~8g
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1½ cups chicken or vegetable stock ~356mL
  • 3 tablespoons sun-dried tomatoes
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste ~4mL

Instructions

  1. Melt butter in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Once the butter is almost melted, add olive oil, shallot, and a pinch of salt–sauté for about 1½ to 2 minutes or until the shallot is mostly translucent.
  2. Stir in the grated garlic and cook for 10 seconds, just until fragrant (we do not want to burn the garlic).
  3. Whisk in the flour and cook for about 1 minute, forming a roux.
  4. Whisk in the stock, sun-dried tomatoes, and ¾ teaspoon salt–bring the heat up to high.
  5. Cook, whisking frequently, until the velouté has significantly thickened (it should be thick enough to fully coat the back of a spoon).
  6. Transfer the velouté to a blender or an immersion blender-friendly vessel (like a deli container). Blend until smooth and silky. Taste and add salt, if needed. Note: ● To Store, once the sauce has cooled completely, place it in an airtight container and store in the refrigerator.
Note● To Store, once the sauce has cooled completely, place it in an airtight container and store in the refrigerator.

PARMESAN MEATBALLS

16 (1½-inch) or 10 (2-inch) meatballs

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • 1 shallot, finely diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, grated ~8g
  • ¼ teaspoon onion powder ~1mL
  • ¼ teaspoon granulated garlic ~1mL
  • ¼ cup panko breadcrumbs ~15g
  • 1 egg ~57g
  • ½ cup grated parmesan
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste ~5mL
  • ½ pound (8-ounce) ground meat (chicken, pork, or beef)

Instructions

  1. Heat a few glugs of olive oil in a sauté pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the diced shallot and a pinch of salt. Once the shallot is translucent, about 1 to 2 minutes, add the grated garlic and stir. Once the garlic is fragrant, after about 15 to 20 seconds of cooking, turn off the heat and transfer the sautéed shallot and garlic to a large mixing bowl.
  2. To the same bowl, add onion powder, garlic powder, breadcrumbs, egg, parmesan, and salt. Stir until well combined, then add the ground meat and mix well.
  3. Heat a thin layer of olive oil in a large skillet over high heat. Using a small spoon or a 1½-inch (½-ounce) cookie scoop, scoop out small rounds of the meat mixture and place directly into the hot oil.
  4. Cook for about 2 to 3 minutes per side until golden brown and crispy on all sides. Once the internal temperature of the largest meatball reaches 165°F, transfer the meatballs to a paper towel-lined plate. Note: ● To bake meatballs, place them on a parchment lined sheet tray, lightly drizzle with olive oil, and bake at 375ºF for 15 to 18 minutes, or until they reach an internal temperature of 165ºF. ● To Store, Allow the meatballs to come to room temperature before transferring them to an airtight container. If you’re consuming them within 4 days, store them in the refrigerator for optimal freshness; for 4+ days, store them in the freezer.
